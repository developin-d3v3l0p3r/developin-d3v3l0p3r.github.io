Meiosis [5.01]

Overview

Meiosis is the process of creating gametes through cell division

It is split into 2 main stages:

  • Meiosis I
  • Meiosis II

Each are split into 4 phases:

  • Prophase
  • Metaphase
  • Anaphase
  • Telophase

Meiosis I

Prophase I

In Prophase I:

  • Chromatin condenses into Chromosomes
  • Spindle fibers attach to the chromosomes at the centromeres
  • Homologous Chromosomes pair up in a process called synapsis, forming tetrads.
  • Once they pair up, they exchange genetic information in a process called crossing over.

    Note:

    The Points where crossing over occurs known as the chiasmata

Metaphase I

Homologous chromosomes align in along the 'equator' of the cell. The paternal and maternal chromosomes line up on either side of the cell, in a process called random assortment.

Note:

This phase could lead to a nondisjunction error if both chromosomes end up on the same side of the cell.

Anaphase I

Each of the homologous chromosomes are pulled towards the opposites sides of the cell.

Telophase I

The nuclear envelope reforms and spindle fibers disappear.
