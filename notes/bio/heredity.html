Meiosis [5.01]

Overview

Meiosis is the process of creating gametes through cell division

It is split into 2 main stages:

  • Meiosis I
  • Meiosis II

Each are split into 4 phases:

  • Prophase
  • Metaphase
  • Anaphase
  • Telophase

Meiosis I

Prophase I

In Prophase I:

  • Chromatin condenses into Chromosomes
  • Homologous Chromosomes pair up in a process called synapsis.
  • Once they pair up, they exchange genetic information in a process called crossing over. tetrads

    Note:

    The Points where crossing over occurs known as the chiasmata

Metaphase I

In Metaphase I:

  • Homologous chromosomes align in along the 'equator' of the cell. The paternal and maternal chromosomes line up on either side of the cell, in a process called random assortment.

    Note:

    This step could lead to a nondisjunction error if both chromosomes end up on the same side of the cell.
